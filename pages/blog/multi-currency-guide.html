Understanding Polban's Multi-Currency Features

December 22, 2024 Polban Team

In today's global economy, many people manage money in multiple currencies. Whether you're an expat, international business owner, or frequent traveler, Polban's multi-currency features make it easy to track all your finances in one place.

What is Multi-Currency Support?

Multi-currency support allows you to record transactions in different currencies while maintaining a unified view of your finances. Polban automatically converts all transactions to your base currency for reporting purposes, giving you a clear picture of your overall financial situation.

Setting Up Your Base Currency

Your base currency is the primary currency used for all reports and analytics. To set it up:

  1. Go to Settings in Polban
  2. Select "Currency Settings"
  3. Choose your preferred base currency (e.g., USD, EUR, IRR)
  4. Save your settings

Creating Multi-Currency Accounts

You can create separate accounts for each currency you use:

  • Bank accounts in different countries
  • Foreign currency wallets
  • International credit cards
  • Investment accounts in various currencies

Recording Multi-Currency Transactions

When you add a transaction, simply:

  1. Select the account with the transaction currency
  2. Enter the amount in the local currency
  3. Polban automatically converts it to your base currency using current exchange rates
  4. Both amounts are saved for your records

Automatic Exchange Rate Updates

Polban regularly updates exchange rates to ensure accurate conversions. This means:

  • Your reports reflect current currency values
  • Historical transactions maintain their original conversion rates
  • You can see how currency fluctuations affect your net worth

Multi-Currency Reporting

All of Polban's reporting features work seamlessly with multi-currency accounts:

  • Balance Sheet: See total assets and liabilities in your base currency
  • Income/Expense Reports: Track earnings and spending across all currencies
  • Net Worth Charts: Visualize your financial growth regardless of currency mix
  • Category Analysis: Compare spending patterns across different currencies

Best Practices for Multi-Currency Management

  1. Use Consistent Account Naming: Include the currency code in account names (e.g., "Bank Account - USD")
  2. Regular Reconciliation: Check your accounts regularly to ensure accuracy
  3. Tag International Transactions: Use tags to identify cross-border expenses
  4. Monitor Exchange Rates: Be aware of significant currency fluctuations
  5. Export Regular Reports: Keep records of your multi-currency finances

Common Use Cases

Expats and Remote Workers

If you earn in one currency but spend in another, Polban helps you track both sides of your finances and understand your real purchasing power.

International Business Owners

Manage revenue and expenses across multiple countries, with automatic consolidation for tax and accounting purposes.

Frequent Travelers

Track travel expenses in local currencies while keeping an eye on your overall budget in your home currency.

Investment Portfolio Management

Monitor international investments and see how currency movements affect your portfolio value.

Conclusion

Polban's multi-currency features eliminate the complexity of managing money across different currencies. With automatic conversions, unified reporting, and detailed tracking, you can focus on making smart financial decisions rather than wrestling with currency calculations.
